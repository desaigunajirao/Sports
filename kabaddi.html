Kabaddi

Rules/Facts about Kabaddi

  1. Objective:
    The objective of Kabaddi is for a raider to score points by tagging or wrestling opponents on the opposing team and returning to their half without being tackled.
  2. Team Composition:
    Each team consists of 7 players on the field, with substitutes allowed to replace players during stoppages.
  3. Raiding:
    A "raider" from the attacking team enters the opponent's half and attempts to tag as many defenders as possible while continuously chanting "Kabaddi" to prove they are not holding their breath.
  4. Chanting Rule:
    The raider must chant "Kabaddi, Kabaddi" without stopping, and if they stop breathing or the chant stops, they are considered "out."
  5. Defending:
    The defenders’ job is to prevent the raider from tagging them and returning to their side of the court. If the raider is held, the defenders earn a point.
